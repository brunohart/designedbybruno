Braun Clock

2025

Time-Based Media · Dieter Rams · p5.js

A digital clock built in the spirit of Dieter Rams. Analog hands, upward-flowing second bars, a minute dot grid, and an AM/PM colour system that shifts between burnt orange mornings and deep blue evenings. The pursuit of clarity through restraint.

Medium
p5.js, vanilla JavaScript
Influence
Dieter Rams, Braun AB 30, Jony Ive
Context
MDDN 242 — Time-Based Media
Intent

As little design as possible

The act of telling time is an interface between human intuition and mechanical precision. This clock does not try to reinvent that relationship. It tries to honour it — rendering time legible, balanced, and quiet.

Every element serves a singular purpose. The analog hands tell the hour. The second bars flow upward with a lerp brightness fade. The minute dots fill in sequence. A soft millisecond glow expands and contracts like breath. The AM/PM indicator is a single dot — burnt orange for morning, deep blue for evening — accompanied by the subtlest typography.

Good design is not about adding complexity; it is about revealing simplicity.
Process

From concept to restraint

The clock began with a more expressive direction — grunged textures, high contrast, visual noise. A deliberate pivot back to Rams' principles stripped it to its essentials. The colour palette reduced to dark grey strokes on light grey, with selective warm and cool tones reserved for the AM/PM system and the red second hand.

The second bars use lerpColor for smooth brightness transitions. The minute dots use a muted blue-grey that reads clearly without demanding attention. The millisecond glow was the final addition — making the smallest unit of time something felt rather than merely seen.
