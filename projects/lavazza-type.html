Lavazza Type System

2025

Parametric Type · p5.js · William Kentridge · Coffee as Parameter

A parametric typeface system where each letter is defined by coffee-themed parameters — crema flow, foam peaks, milk body, tilt angle. Three independent Bezier curves per glyph, colour-lerped between espresso, oat milk, and americano, animated through a three-phase interpolation that feels like cream cutting through a fresh pour.

Origin

From espresso to alphabet

The project started with a Lavazza capsule box and a Mubi screening of William Kentridge's My Life as a Coffee Pot. Kentridge's charcoal flipbook animations — imperfect, smoky, alive — suggested that type could breathe the same way. The capsule's geometry, the machine's spout arcs, and the thick creamy script on the packaging became the formal vocabulary.

Three master shapes emerged: a thick espresso-brown pour line, a soft oat-milk cream swirl, and a rich americano-black stroke. Every letter is composed from these three curves, each with independently programmable thickness, rotation, wavelength, and amplitude.

Interpolation

The pour between letters

When letters transition, they don't simply morph. They pass through a three-phase interpolation designed to feel like coffee being poured:

Phase 1 (0-20%): The old letter dissolves toward a neutral "default" state — like clearing the cup.

Phase 2 (20-80%): A crema blob swells across all three curves, foam height and milk body boosting in a sine wave. Three staggered droplets — espresso, oat milk, crema — leap into the letterform. A colour flash signals the blob's apex.

Phase 3 (80-100%): The new letter emerges from the default, settling into its final form with a subtle 5% scale pulse — a latte art heartbeat.
